## Load Bearing
Remove this piece and the DDR has no institutional framework for governing its transport network on 7 October 1949. The Deutsche Reichsbahn exists as an operating entity. The road network exists as physical infrastructure. Without the MfV, there is no ministry to coordinate them, no position through which the state can govern the network, and no institutional counterpart to the Soviet transport authority. Every subsequent minister inherits the structure Reingruber built from the Hauptverwaltung Verkehr of the Deutsche Wirtschaftskommission.

## Apparent
A ministry established by decree on 7 October 1949 as part of the first Grotewohl cabinet. Housed at Franzosische Strasse 53-56, Berlin-Mitte. Staff drawn from the Deutsche Wirtschaftskommission Hauptverwaltung Verkehr, which Reingruber had led since 1948. Organizational structure: departments for rail, road, waterway, and aviation, mirroring the Reichsverkehrsministerium structure Reingruber had worked in from 1916 to 1933. One minister, parteilos. One ministry. One network, divided by occupation zone borders.

## Yearbook

**1948:** Reingruber appointed to lead the Hauptverwaltung Verkehr of the Deutsche Wirtschaftskommission in the Soviet Occupation Zone. This is the administrative predecessor of the MfV. The structure and staff transfer directly.

**7 October 1949:** DDR founded. Ministerrat constituted under Minister-President Otto Grotewohl. Reingruber appointed Minister fur Verkehr, parteilos. He is the only minister in a core infrastructure portfolio without SED membership.

**1949-1950:** Ministry establishes operational authority over Deutsche Reichsbahn within DDR territory. The Reichsbahn remains a single legal entity spanning both German states. West German Bundesbahn not yet formally separated. Reingruber governs the DDR portion of a network that still has no legal border.

**1950:** Reichsbahn formally divided. Deutsche Reichsbahn (DDR) and Deutsche Bundesbahn (BRD) operate as separate entities. The border between them runs through active rail junctions. Reingruber's ministry now governs a truncated network under Soviet freight priority obligations.

**1950-1952:** Reingruber serves concurrently as dean of the faculty of civil engineering at TH Dresden. The ministry is not his only institutional home. He maintains the academic position throughout his ministerial tenure.

**1952:** Diploma programme in traffic engineering introduced at the Hochschule fur Verkehrswesen Dresden, later named after Friedrich List. The curriculum Reingruber shaped as an academic now trains the engineers who will staff the ministry and the Reichsbahn for the next generation.

**30 April 1953:** Reingruber leaves office. Official reason: health. He is 64. He returns to TH Dresden as professor. Roman Chwalek succeeds him. The ministry he founded continues without him. He receives an honorary doctorate from the Hochschule fur Verkehrswesen Dresden the same year.

**1953 onwards:** Reingruber teaches at TH Dresden until 1964. The ministry structure he built in 1949 remains the institutional framework for DDR transport governance until 1990.
