# Bundesminister fur Verkehr (1949-1998)

## Owner
- **Project:** REMEMBER
- **Process:** [Govern Traffic](../processes/process_govern_traffic.md)
- **Place:** [Bundesverkehrsministerium Bonn, Robert-Schuman-Platz 1](../places/place_bonn_robert_schuman_platz.md)

## Has
Constitutional mandate to govern federal roads, rail, waterways, aviation, and shipping. Second-largest ministry budget in the federal government. Direct authority over Deutsche Bundesbahn and Lufthansa. 44 subordinate agencies. A ministry staff that predates the minister and will outlast them.

## Orders
Govern the network without losing the coalition.

## Loses
The certainty that the position is about transport. It is about survival inside a coalition, inside a party, inside a government that has other priorities. The minister who arrives believing the job is infrastructure discovers it is politics first and infrastructure second.

## Drives
The minister either adapts - subordinating the network to the coalition - or commits to a piece that forces the coalition to follow. Most adapt. A few commit. The ones who commit are remembered.
