# Minister fur Verkehrswesen (DDR 1949-1990)

## Owner
- **Project:** REMEMBER
- **Process:** [Govern Traffic](../processes/process_govern_traffic.md)
- **Place:** [Ministerium fur Verkehrswesen DDR, Franzosische Strasse 53-56](../places/place_mfv_ddr_franzosische_strasse.md)

## Has
Mandate to govern the transport network of the Deutsche Demokratische Republik: Deutsche Reichsbahn, inland waterways, road network, civil aviation. Direct authority over the largest single employer in the DDR. A ministry staff drawn from the pre-1945 Reichsbahn and Reichsverkehrsministerium bureaucracy. Formally: ministerial rank in the Ministerrat der DDR, appointed by the Volkskammer, accountable to the Ministerrat.

Structurally: the position exists within a single-party state. The SED holds the governing authority. The minister governs traffic. Whether the minister is a party member determines how much the position is the role and how much it is a function.

## Orders
Keep the network running in service of the planned economy.

## Loses
The assumption that technical competence is sufficient. The network is not an engineering problem. It is a political instrument. Freight priorities are set by the plan, not by the network's capacity. The Reichsbahn serves Soviet reparations before it serves DDR passengers. The minister who arrives as an engineer discovers that the timetable is not his to write.

## Drives
The position produces ministers who either subordinate the network to the plan and survive, or treat the network as a technical system with its own logic and are replaced. The ones who last longest are the ones who stop distinguishing between the two.
