# Hans Reingruber

## Owner
- **Project:** REMEMBER

## Title
[Minister fur Verkehrswesen (DDR 1949-1990)](../positions/position_minister_fur_verkehrswesen_ddr_1949_1990.md)
*In office: 7 October 1949 - 30 April 1953. First Minister fur Verkehr of the DDR. Parteilos.*

## Projection
A civil engineer from Wuppertal, born 1888, trained at the TH Hannover, shaped by thirty years inside the Prussian and then Reich transport bureaucracy. Compact, precise in speech, accustomed to institutional structures that outlast the governments they serve. In photographs from the Volkskammer sessions: dark suit, white shirt, the bearing of a professor who has been in rooms like this before and expects to leave them when the session ends. Holds the [Minister fur Verkehrswesen](../positions/position_minister_fur_verkehrswesen_ddr_1949_1990.md) as the first person to occupy it - a position without precedent, in a state without precedent, built on institutions he already knows.

## Action
Reingruber converts existing administrative structure into ministerial form. The Hauptverwaltung Verkehr of the Deutsche Wirtschaftskommission, which he had run since 1948, becomes the [MfV Foundation](../pieces/piece_mfv_foundation.md): same staff, same files, new letterhead, new legal authority. He does not build from nothing. He formalizes what already exists and makes it governable. The ministry he hands to Chwalek in 1953 is functional, staffed, and institutionally legible: the only kind of inheritance a technical man considers complete.

## Shadow
Reingruber understands institutions as engineering systems: design them correctly and they function regardless of who operates them. He refused to join the NSDAP in 1933 and was permitted to continue working. He does not join the SED in 1949 and is again permitted to continue working. In both cases the permission is granted because he is more useful inside the institution than outside it. He does not account for the possibility that an institution correctly designed for one political system becomes a precise instrument of control in another. The ministry he founded to govern traffic governs traffic efficiently for forty years: under conditions he did not design and would not have chosen.

He leaves in April 1953, officially for health reasons. He is 64. The timing places his departure six weeks before the workers' uprising of 17 June 1953. Whether the sequence is coincidence or calculation is not in any file.

## Tell
When the ministry's scope is questioned, Reingruber specifies the technical requirement. He does not address the political question.
