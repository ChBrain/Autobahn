# Hans-Christoph Seebohm

## Owner
- **Project:** REMEMBER

## Title
[Bundesminister fur Verkehr (1949-1998)](../positions/position_bundesminister_fuer_verkehr_1949_1998.md)
*In office: 20 September 1949 - 30 November 1966. Longest uninterrupted tenure of any federal minister in BRD history: 6,219 days.*

## Projection
A mining engineer from Silesia, born 1903, displaced twice: once by the war, once by the expulsion. Tall, formal, technically precise. Speaks with the authority of someone who has spent twenty years building things underground and expects the same discipline above it. Arrives at cabinet meetings with prepared positions and does not easily revise them. In photographs from the 1950s: dark suit, reading glasses on the desk, a man who treats the ministry as an engineering project with a 30-year timeline. Holds the [Bundesminister fur Verkehr](../positions/position_bundesminister_fuer_verkehr_1949_1998.md) longer than any other person ever will.

## Action
Seebohm converts political mandate into engineering output. Every brief becomes a specification, every coalition instruction becomes a law, every budget line becomes concrete and asphalt. The [Federal Motorway Network + Road Transport Regulation](../pieces/piece_federal_motorway_network.md) is the record of this: 17 years of converting governance into infrastructure, including the truck restriction laws so precise they forced the industry to innovate against its will. On weekends, he gives speeches for the Sudetendeutsche Landsmannschaft about the right of return to Czechoslovakia. Adenauer hopes the workload will keep him too busy. It does not.

## Shadow
Seebohm believes his technical authority protects him from political consequence. He treats the ministry as a domain of engineering and the rest of government as noise to be managed. He cannot see that his weekend speeches: his irredentist claims about the Sudetenland, his nationalist rhetoric at expellee gatherings, are not a private matter tolerated by the government. They are a liability accumulating interest. He is certain the roads speak for themselves. He does not understand that a minister is not only what he builds.

Der Spiegel observed that he treated cabinet decisions as obstacles to be circumvented whenever a political platform presented itself. He reads this as a technical misunderstanding. It is not.

## Tell
When challenged on his expellee speeches, Seebohm cites kilometres of motorway built, not the content of what was said.
