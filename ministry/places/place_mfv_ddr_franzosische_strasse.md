# Ministerium fur Verkehrswesen DDR, Franzosische Strasse 53-56, Berlin-Mitte

## Owner
- **Project:** REMEMBER
- **Place:** Deutsche Demokratische Republik / Ministerrat

## Shown
A Wilhelmine office building in Berlin-Mitte, Franzosische Strasse 53-56, two blocks south of Unter den Linden. Five storeys, sandstone facade, internal courtyards. Built late nineteenth century, adapted for administrative use. The street runs east-west through the former Prussian government quarter. To the north: the Staatsoper. To the east: the Berliner Dom, three hundred metres. To the west: the Ministerrat building on the Marx-Engels-Platz, four hundred metres.

## Holds
- [MfV Foundation](../pieces/piece_mfv_foundation.md)
- Minister's office
- Reichsbahn liaison directorate
- Soviet transport authority liaison desk, active until 1955
- Files on reparations freight obligations, classified
- Timetable coordination with Polish and Czech border authorities

## Offers
A minister can issue operational directives to the Deutsche Reichsbahn from here. Coordinate freight allocation with the Staatliche Plankommission. Receive Soviet transport authority delegations. Sign bilateral rail agreements with fraternal socialist states. Set the DDR's position in OSJD negotiations: the Organisation for Cooperation of Railways, the socialist counterpart to UIC.

## Withheld
The reparations obligation. Soviet freight movements through DDR territory take priority over DDR domestic operations. This is not in any ministerial brief. It is in the operating reality of every Reichsbahn dispatcher. The minister governs the network that is left after that obligation is met. The gap between what the position offers and what the position actually controls is not written down anywhere in this building.
