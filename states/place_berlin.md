# Berlin
## Die Stadt, deren Identität auf Volatilität gebaut ist.

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Shown
The A115 from the north: the Grunewald forest pressing close on both sides. Two lanes each direction, the narrowest section of any Berlin motorway. No city visible - only trees. Then Dreieck Funkturm: the Funkturm itself briefly in view between the junction approaches. The road widens. Urban.

The A111 descending into Tunnel Tegel - the road going underground where the airport runway once crossed overhead. The tunnel built before the airport was demolished. The runway gone. The tunnel continues.

Speed limits throughout. The Berlin road network never unrestricted.

## Holds
- [the A111](../roads/a111/place_the_a111.md) - Waidmannsluster Damm to Dreieck Funkturm, via Tunnel Tegel
- [the A115](../roads/a115/place_the_a115.md) - AVUS, Dreieck Funkturm to Kreuz Zehlendorf and the Brandenburg border

## Offers
Speed limits on all motorways - typically 100 km/h, lower in tunnels and at junctions. Dense exits. Short distances between junctions. The AVUS on the A115: historically a racing circuit, now a federal motorway with traffic lights at the northern end.

## Withheld
The cobblestone line in the pavement across the city - 155 km of double cobblestones marking where the Wall ran. Not visible from the motorway. Checkpoint Bravo 600 metres south of Kreuz Zehlendorf on the A115: the former Allied control point for the West Berlin transit corridor. The buildings still stand in the forest. The motorway passes them without showing them.
