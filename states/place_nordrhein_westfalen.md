# Nordrhein-Westfalen
## Die Solidarität, die die Republik gebaut hat und sich jetzt selbst neu aufbaut.

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Shown
The road widens. Trucks in the right lane continuous. The Ruhr valley on both sides of the A40: the former collieries, the gasometers, the cooling towers now repurposed. At Essen-Mülheim on the A40: the Stadtbahn track running in the Mittelstreifen between the carriageways. Two modes of transport on the same alignment, aware of each other.

The A40 westbound at peak hour: the most congested Autobahn in Germany by frequency. The infrastructure of a post-industrial region that moved goods by road when it moved goods by everything.

## Holds
- [the A40](../roads/a40/place_the_a40.md) - Essen to Dortmund, Ruhrschnellweg

## Offers
100 km/h through urban sections of the A40. High truck density. The road surface older in places - NRW maintains the highest volume network in Germany. Congestion at predictable points: Kreuz Kaiserberg (A40/A3), Kreuz Duisburg (A40/A59), Dreieck Herne (A40/A43). The road is aware of the volume. The volume is not surprised by the road.

## Withheld
The Zeche Zollverein in Essen - UNESCO World Heritage site, visible as a silhouette from the A40. The Gasometer Oberhausen 2 km from the A40 at Oberhausen. The Rhine between Köln and Düsseldorf: 20 km from most of the A40, not visible from it. What was made here and is no longer made here.
