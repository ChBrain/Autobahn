# A261

## Die Eckverbindung.

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Shown
Nine kilometres of two-lane motorway connecting the A7 at Dreieck Hamburg-Südwest to the A1 at Buchholzer Dreieck. Built 1979-1983 as a corner connector - the Eckverbindung - specifically to relieve the village streets of Nenndorf and Tötensen of the freight load that built up after the Elbtunnel opened in 1975. The road descends from the Hamburg city edge through the Harburger Berge into the Nordheide. Two lanes throughout. No service stop.

## Offers
The A7 northwest toward Flensburg and southeast toward Hannover. The A1 northeast toward Hamburg and southwest toward Bremen. The only motorway connection between the two main Hamburg corridors on the city's southwestern side.

## Withheld
The road passes through the Harburger Berge - an Ice Age moraine landscape whose boundaries have been marked with standing stones since the 14th century. Of the original approximately 246 boundary stones of the Staatsforst Rosengarten, 176 survive along forest tracks directly beside the A261 corridor, listed as cultural monuments. The Wildpark Schwarze Berge - 1,000 animals, 100 species, wolves, lynx, bears, elk and wisent - lies 4 km west of the Tötensen exit. The Hünengrab in the Kleckerwald near Buchholz - 5,200 years old, 48 metres long, 76 standing granite stones - is 5 km from the southern terminus. The road passes all of it. None of it is on a sign.
