# Tötensen (Rosengarten)

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn
- Place: [A261](place_a261.md) in [Niedersachsen](place_niedersachsen.md)

## Shown
The Tötensen exit. The sign: Rosengarten-Tötensen. The road passing through the Harburger Berge - forested hills, the landscape dropping away from the carriageway. The Hamburg boundary already 3 km to the north. Niedersachsen throughout.

## Holds
[km 4.9](piece_the_kilometerstein.md): Tötensen (Rosengarten).
- 3.3 km north: [Hamburg-Marmstorf/Lürade](place_a261_hamburg_marmstorf.md)
- 4.5 km south: [Buchholzer Dreieck](place_a261_buchholzer_dreieck.md)

## Offers
Ausfahrt - the exit ramp. Tötensen and the Rosengarten landscape below.

## Withheld
The Wildpark Schwarze Berge - over 1,000 animals across around 100 species, including wolves, lynx, bears, elk and wisent, in the Harburger Berge woodland - lies 4 km north of the exit. The park's own directions name this exit as the A261 access point. It is Hamburg's closest large wildlife park, open 365 days a year. The road does not indicate it is there.
