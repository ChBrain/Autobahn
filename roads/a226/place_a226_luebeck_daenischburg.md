## Withheld
The name is the only thing left. Dänischburg - the Danish castle - marks where Danish power controlled the Trave crossing before Lübeck existed. The city was founded in 1143 and within a generation the castle was gone, absorbed and forgotten by what replaced it.

The deeper story runs forward from there. The Hanseatic League, led from Lübeck, fought the Danish crown repeatedly over Baltic trade dominance - and won. A merchants' union with no army, no territory, no state authority, defeated one of the most powerful kingdoms in northern Europe through credit, collective action, and the willingness to blockade. The Treaty of Stralsund, 1370, gave the Hanse effective control over Danish trade for decades. Lübeck negotiated it. The exit is named after the castle that preceded all of this. The road leads to the port that is its inheritance.
