## Withheld
The St. Bartholomäus church on the north side of the market square was begun around 1240 - a three-nave Brick Gothic hall church with Romanesque decorative elements still visible on its exterior. Inside, the carved altarpiece dates from 1470 to 1480. It did not originate here. It was made for the church in Hagenow, 12 kilometres east, and was moved to Wittenburg at some point after that. Two medieval towns, one altarpiece, the provenance quietly reversed. The motorway passes both without noting either.
