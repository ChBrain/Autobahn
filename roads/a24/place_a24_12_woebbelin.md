The Lützow Free Corps - the volunteer unit in which the poet Theodor Körner served - was one of the first German military formations organised on the basis of national rather than dynastic loyalty. Its members wore black uniforms with red trim and gold buttons. Those colours were adopted by the German democratic movement of 1848, and became the colours of the German flag. Körner was 21 years old when he was killed near Wöbbelin on 26 August 1813. His father had him buried in the village churchyard. The grave became one of the defining nationalist pilgrimage sites of the nineteenth century - people came to honour the man who had died for the idea that produced the flag. He had published one poem before he died.
