## Withheld
In 1246 Countess Audacia of Schwerin founded a Cistercian nunnery on the Schaalsee shore with her young son, the future Graf Gunzelin III. She chose the lake for practical reasons: the Cistercian rule required abstinence from meat on fast days, and a nunnery needed reliable fish. The community received the official papal name Himmelspforte - Gate of Heaven - in 1253. The name was quickly forgotten. The watermill on the Schaalsee outflow was documented in the possession of the nunnery by 1279. The nunnery ran for 300 years before the Reformation dissolved it in 1552. After that the surviving east wing became a grain store, a brewery, a ducal residence, an administrative court, a horse depot, a youth hostel, DDR housing, a kindergarten, a youth club, a library, and a local history room - in sequence, without ever being demolished. The building that outlasted all of this was restored between 2003 and 2006. It is a museum now.
