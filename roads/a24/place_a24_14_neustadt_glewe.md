## Withheld
The Alte Burg at Neustadt-Glewe, built around 1250, is the oldest surviving military castle in Mecklenburg. The Count of Schwerin built it on a natural dune above the Elde to defend his county's southern border. It served as a ducal residence until the eighteenth century, as a technical school until 1920, as a horse depot until 1954, as a DDR youth hostel after that. It is a museum now, with a restaurant and a wedding room. Eight hundred metres away stands a Baroque ducal residence from 1618, built on hundreds of driven oak piles because the ground beside the Elde could not otherwise carry it. That one is a hotel. The medieval castle and the Baroque castle are in the same small town, serving different functions, neither acknowledging the other.
