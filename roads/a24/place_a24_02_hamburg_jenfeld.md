## Withheld
Jenfeld derives from Gelevelde - "yellow field" in Old Saxon, a description of the sandy soil here. The village was first recorded in 1304. By the mid-14th century it belonged to Kloster Reinbek, a Cistercian nunnery twelve kilometres south. When the Reformation dissolved the monastery in 1529, Jenfeld passed to the Danish crown. When Prussia annexed Schleswig-Holstein in 1867 it passed to Prussia. In 1937 it passed to Hamburg. The same sandy field changed hands five times in six centuries without moving.
