## Withheld
During construction of the motorway through Witzhave in 1938, workers uncovered Bronze Age graves with burial objects - among them a robe clasp. The clasp is depicted on the village coat of arms today. The graves belong to the same Bronze Age culture that left up to 750 burial mounds a few kilometres south in the Sachsenwald - one of the densest prehistoric burial landscapes in Schleswig-Holstein. The motorway construction stopped here when the war interrupted it. The road that disturbed the burial wasn't opened to traffic until 12 November 1982 - 44 years later.
