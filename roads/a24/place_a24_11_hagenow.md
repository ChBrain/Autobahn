## Withheld
Hagenow Land station opened on 15 October 1846 on the first railway line in Mecklenburg. On 1 May 1847 - six months later - it became the first railway junction in Mecklenburg when the branch to Schwerin opened. The station building had a Prussian side and a Mecklenburg side, the Berlin-Hamburg main line under Prussian ownership, the Schwerin branch under the Grand Duchy. In 1985 a semi-automatic marshalling yard with ten sorting tracks was built here. After reunification the yard had no function - the East-West freight flows it had regulated no longer required regulation at this point. The yard was closed. The site is being renaturated since 2023. The station building is listed, empty, and deteriorating.
