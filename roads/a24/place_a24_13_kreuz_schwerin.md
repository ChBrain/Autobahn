## Withheld
Two kilometres west at Wöbbelin, in the final weeks of the war, a subcamp of KZ Neuengamme held several thousand prisoners in severely overcrowded conditions. Hundreds died. American forces liberated it on 2 May 1945 - three days before the German surrender. General James Gavin ordered the townspeople of Ludwigslust - 10 kilometres north on the A14 - to exhume the bodies of those who had died and rebury them in the Schlosspark, the park of the palace built around a hunting lodge. The graves remain there. The junction that connects these places was built thirty years later.
