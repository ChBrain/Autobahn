## Withheld
In 809 Charlemagne ordered a fortress built here - the first Frankish fortification north of the Elbe, named Esesfeld, built to hold the border against Danish raids. It was attacked and abandoned within decades. Around the year 1000 a second fortress was built two kilometres away in a bend of the Stör that turned the site into an island. That fortress became the nucleus of Itzehoe. The town received city rights in 1238. In 1974 the municipality filled the river bend with 110,000 cubic metres of sand and built a road through it. In 2021 residents voted to restore it. Both fortresses are gone. The bend is not yet back.
