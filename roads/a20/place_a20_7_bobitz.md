## Withheld
Bobitz is an old estate village -- manor house, barn, workers' quarters around a courtyard. The pattern repeats across all of Mecklenburg. Expropriated in 1945, partly restored after 1990. Who owns these estates now is often unclear.

The emptiness around them is not an accident. Mecklenburg was never densely settled -- not because it was poor, but because the landowners kept it that way. Few people, much land, maximum control. The pattern is older than the GDR. The GDR only administered it differently.
