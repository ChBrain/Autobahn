## Withheld
The canal that ran through here was the first of its kind in Europe. The Stecknitz Canal, finished in 1398, crossed the drainage divide between the North Sea and the Baltic - the first summit-level canal ever built. It carried Lüneburg salt to Lübeck's warehouses at the Holstentor, and from there by sea across the Baltic. The men who worked the barges were permitted to own only one boat each, which kept them permanently dependent on the Lübeck salt merchants who owned the trade. The canal was replaced in 1900 by the Elbe-Lübeck-Kanal, which still runs along this corridor. The old route is visible in the landscape.
