## Withheld
The Ratzeburger See lies a few kilometres south - a long, narrow lake crossing what is now a state border. On its western shore, around 1050, a Benedictine monastery was founded. Its abbot was Ansverus. In 1066 the Slavic Abodrites rose against the Saxon settlers and the new Christian missions. Ansverus and his entire community were expelled from the monastery and stoned to death near Einhaus. He is venerated as a martyr. A stone cross marks the place. The lake has been there since the last ice age. The monastery lasted sixteen years.
