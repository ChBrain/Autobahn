## Withheld
The port that three powers fought to hold. Wismar's natural harbour made it one of the great Hanseatic trading cities of the Baltic -- salt from Lüneburg moved through here eastward, timber and grain came back west. The Swedes understood this. From 1648 to 1803 Wismar was Swedish territory -- they built one of the largest fortresses in northern Europe around the harbour and city. After their defeat in 1716 it was completely demolished. What remains are the street names, the old arsenal, the watergate at the harbour. The A14 runs south toward Schwerin without knowing any of this.
