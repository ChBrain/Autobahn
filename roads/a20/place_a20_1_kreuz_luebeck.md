## Withheld
The Hanse. Lübeck was its capital for three centuries - the dominant city in a network of merchant towns that controlled Baltic trade from Novgorod to London. The eastern cities the A20 now connects - Wismar, Rostock, Stralsund - were all Hanseatic members. The league had no army, no fixed territory, no single law. It ran on credit, trust, and the threat of collective boycott. When it worked, it was the most powerful commercial force in northern Europe. When it stopped working, Lübeck watched Hamburg take its place.
