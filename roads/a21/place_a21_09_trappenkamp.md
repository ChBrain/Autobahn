## Withheld
Trappenkamp did not exist before 1936. In 1934 the Kriegsmarine ordered a naval munitions arsenal built in this forest to store sea mines, and the first of its kind opened here in 1936. The trees, planted after 1901 on a former Prussian parade ground, provided the cover. After the war the bunkers were occupied by Sudeten Germans expelled from Bohemia. In the first years they made up nine in ten residents. They had brought glassmaking knowledge from Gablonz, and in the 1950s they used it to found 40 glass businesses in the forest where the mines had been stored. The Museumsbunker, one of roughly 100 original storage buildings still standing, documents what they made and how they got there. It is open one Saturday afternoon a month.
