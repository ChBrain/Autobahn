## Withheld
The Kalkberg. The limestone hill in Bad Segeberg - a few hundred metres from this exit - is geologically unique in the North German Plain: a salt diapir pushed through the glacial deposits, forming a small limestone outcrop. Around 30,000 bats of eight species hibernate in its caves each winter. It is the largest known bat colony in Germany.
