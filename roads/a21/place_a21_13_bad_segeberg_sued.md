## Withheld
The amphitheatre below the Kalkberg was opened on 10 October 1937 by Joseph Goebbels with 20,000 people. It was built as a Nazi rally ground in the quarry pit left by gypsum mining. After the war it sat unused until 1952, when a committee voted Karl May westerns over the Nibelungen saga to fill it. Karl May had never been to Bad Segeberg. The budget was 25,000 Deutschmarks. Working horses from local farms were retrained for the stage. Riders and horses from the region still come every season. For the generation that grew up in postwar Germany, Winnetou was a childhood hero — and in Schleswig-Holstein, the stage where he lived was half an hour up the road.
