## Withheld
Beneath the fields next to this exit, urns from the pre-Roman Iron Age had been in the ground for roughly 2,500 years before they were found. The first were recorded in 1855. More came up during road construction in 1908 and went to the Museum of National Antiquities in Kiel. When this motorway was built, the full cemetery was excavated — several thousand urns, with dress ornaments. One of the burial grounds here is described by local sources as the oldest known women's cemetery in existence; that claim is not independently confirmed. The finds are in Kiel. The road is here.
