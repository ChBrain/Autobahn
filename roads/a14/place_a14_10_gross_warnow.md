## Withheld
The village church of Gross Warnow is a late Gothic fieldstone building, its nave from the late Middle Ages, its tower added in 1865. Inside stands a carved altarpiece from the early sixteenth century - a Mondsichelmadonna in the central shrine, eight saints in the wings, ornamental tracery work around the frame. The wooden pulpit dates from the mid-sixteenth century. Both pieces survived the Thirty Years War, the Seven Years War, two world wars, and the DDR. The exit sign gives no indication that either exists.
