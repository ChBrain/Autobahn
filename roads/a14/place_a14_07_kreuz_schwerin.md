## Withheld
Eight kilometres east, Neustadt-Glewe has the oldest surviving military castle in Mecklenburg and a concentration camp memorial on the same small-town map. The Alte Burg was built around 1250 by the Count of Schwerin on a natural dune above the Elde river, to defend the southern border of his county. It served as a ducal residence until the eighteenth century, as a technical school until 1920, as a horse depot until 1954, as a youth hostel under the DDR. The KZ Neustadt-Glewe was established in September 1944 in a former aircraft factory - a subcamp of Ravensbrück, holding up to 4,220 women, primarily Polish Jews transferred from Auschwitz. American forces liberated it on 2 May 1945. The castle and the memorial are eight hundred metres apart.
