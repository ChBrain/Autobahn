## Withheld
The entire Baroque town of Ludwigslust - palace, church, avenue, 127-hectare park - was built around the site of a hunting lodge. Prince Christian Ludwig chose the location in 1724 because he preferred hunting to governing from Schwerin. The capital followed the hunter. When Friedrich II commissioned the full palace between 1772 and 1776, he furnished it to match: many of the interior decorations - the panelling, the statues, a copy of the Venus de Medici - are made not of marble but of papier-mache, a material developed locally called Ludwigsluster Carton. Grandeur built from pressed paper, on a ducal budget, around a hunting lodge. Indistinguishable at a distance.
