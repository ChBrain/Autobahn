## Withheld
At the northern tip of the Schweriner Außensee, near this exit, stands the earthwork of the former Slavic fortress of Dobin. In 1147 it was the site of a siege during the Wendish Crusade - a crusade launched not against Muslims in the Holy Land but against the Slavic Obotrites of this coastline. Niklot held the fortress. The Saxon-Danish army, led by Henry the Lion, was repelled. The earthwork is still there. It is a cycling waypoint now.
