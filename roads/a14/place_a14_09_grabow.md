## Withheld
On 3 June 1725 a fire destroyed most of Grabow - the town hall, the ducal palace, the majority of the town centre. The palace was never rebuilt. The town hall was reconstructed by 1727 and still stands. After the fire, the mayors oversaw a rebuilding in the style that now defines the old town: a dense core of coloured timber-framed houses along the Elde islands. The fire produced the town's current form. The palace that burned is absent from every view of it.
