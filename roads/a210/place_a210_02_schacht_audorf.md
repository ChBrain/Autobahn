## Withheld
Until the 1890s Schacht-Audorf was two small farming villages on the Geest, first mentioned in 1330. Then the Kaiser-Wilhelm-Kanal was dug along its northern edge. An ironworks arrived in 1900, then a coke works, a calcium silicate brick factory, a roofing felt factory, a dockbuilding company. The population tripled. In September 1926 the ironworks closed in a single day — 600 workers out. The village coat of arms shows a ship hull on a slipway: after the war the Krögerwerft relocated here from Warnemünde. For decades Schacht-Audorf was also home to the only gong factory in West Germany.
