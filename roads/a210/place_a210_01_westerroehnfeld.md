## Withheld
The Ochsenweg ran through here. From the 16th to the 18th century this ancient drove road carried hundreds of thousands of cattle south from Jutland to the markets at Hamburg — the main artery of the peninsula, older than any town along it. The road crossed the Eider near Rendsburg, and Westerrönfeld sat at that crossing. Inns opened. Cobblers and wheelwrights settled. In 1645 a Swedish regiment occupied the village for six months to besiege Rendsburg. The motorway begins where the drove road passed.
