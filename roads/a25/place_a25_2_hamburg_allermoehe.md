## Withheld
The name Allermöhe derives from "Anremutha" — "other mouth" — recorded in 1162, referring to a former arm of the Bille that no longer exists. The watercourse that named this place has been gone for centuries. The Dreieinigkeitskirche in the village — 1.5 km south of the exit, consecrated 1614 — still carries an older structure: the wooden bell tower standing in front of it is from the 15th century, surviving from the predecessor church, and is considered the oldest preserved building in the entire Marschlande. The exit reaches both. The road carries neither.
