## Withheld
In 1865 Alfred Nobel bought land on the Krümmel hill above Geesthacht and built his first factory outside Sweden - producing liquid nitroglycerin under the name "Blasting Oil." The factory exploded in 1866, was rebuilt, exploded again in 1870, was rebuilt again. After the first explosion Nobel anchored a raft in the Elbe and set up a provisional laboratory on board. The dynamite patent came in 1867. Swedish surnames - Bengtsson, Pettersson, Johansson - still run through Geesthacht families descended from the workers Nobel brought from Sweden.
