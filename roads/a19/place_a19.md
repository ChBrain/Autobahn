# A19
## Ostseeautobahn - Rostock an der Warnow bis Röbel, das Warnowtunnel als einzige Mautautobahn Deutschlands

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Shown
The tolled tunnel: 790 metres under the Warnow, the only section of German Autobahn requiring toll for all vehicles. The road emerges north of the Baltic port of Rostock, crosses A20 Kreuz Rostock as Europastraße 55, and threads south through Mecklenburg-Vorpommern toward Berlin: the lakes district, the Müritz, the DDR-era infrastructure completed in 1978 after eight years of construction by the VEB Autobahnbaukombinat. The road straight through flat forest and water. The ferries visible from Rostock exits - Denmark, Sweden, Finland, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania all accessible from the port.

## Offers
Direct access to the Baltic port of Rostock and its international ferry connections (Denmark, Sweden, Finland, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania). The Müritz national park and lakes, the Pommerscher Landrücken as distant geography. The toll tunnel as punctuation - eastbound ferry traffic, westbound Berlin traffic. EU border crossing implicit: Baltic states, Scandinavia, Berlin.

## Withheld
The 24 kilometre gap between Röbel and Wittstock/Dosse - longest exit-free Autobahn stretch in Germany. The planned exit Wredenhagen never built. The DDR-era political decision to connect the port to Berlin, made under Ulbricht in 1959, suspended at the Wall, resumed after 1989. The sandsturm disaster of April 8, 2011 near Kavelstorf - 85 vehicles, 8 dead, the weather weaponised by landscape. The ferries and their flags: only from the port, not from the road itself.
