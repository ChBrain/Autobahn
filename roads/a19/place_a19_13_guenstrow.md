## Withheld
Güstrow Cathedral was begun in 1226 and not consecrated until 1335 - 109 years spanning the shift from Romanesque to Gothic, both visible in the same building. In 1927 Ernst Barlach completed the Floating Angel, a bronze figure suspended inside it as a memorial to the dead of World War I. The Nazis seized it in 1937 and melted it down for ammunition in 1941. Barlach had made a duplicate and given it to a friend who hid it. A cast of that hidden copy now hangs in the cathedral. The original became bullets. Barlach was banned from working, denounced by his neighbours, and died in Güstrow in 1938. The exit is two kilometres from the cathedral.
