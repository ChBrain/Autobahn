## Withheld
In 2003, Rostock hosted the IGA - the Internationale Gartenausstellung, the International Garden Exhibition. Three million visitors came. The exhibition site stretched along the western Warnow shore, immediately adjacent to this district. It was the largest single investment in Rostock's public infrastructure since reunification and the city's deliberate statement about what it wanted to become: not only a port, not only a DDR memory, but a destination. The gardens were temporary. The promenade they built along the river is still there.
