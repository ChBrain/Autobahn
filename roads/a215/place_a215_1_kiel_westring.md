Each June the Kieler Förde hosts the largest sailing regatta in the world - Kieler Woche, held since 1882, three million visitors over nine days. The same water, the same city. On 3 November 1918 sailors refused orders for a suicidal last battle against the British fleet. Seven demonstrators were shot dead on the streets a few kilometres east. By evening Kiel was in the hands of the uprising. Within days the Kaiser had abdicated. Germany's republic began here - started by sailors who knew the sea well enough to know the war was lost.
