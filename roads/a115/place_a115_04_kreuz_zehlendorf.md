# Kreuz Zehlendorf

## Shown
Exit 4. km 16.8. A cloverleaf - four ramps, 12 hectares, completed 1940. The B1 crosses the A115 here: Potsdamer Chaussee from west to east. The sign: Zehlendorf, B1, Potsdam. Toward the north: three lanes begin here again - the narrow two-lane section through Grunewald ends. Nikolassee lies to the east, not visible through the forest.

- Toward Dreieck Funkturm: [Spanische Allee (3)](place_a115_03_spanische_allee.md), 1.5 km.
- Toward Dreieck Nuthetal: Kleinmachnow (5), 1.3 km. State border Berlin-Brandenburg, 0.6 km to the south.

## Offers
Exit 4 - the ramp. Zehlendorf, B1 toward Potsdam or toward the city center. For drivers from Leipzig or Brandenburg: the first real Berlin exit after the border.

## Withheld
Checkpoint Bravo. Immediately behind this junction - on the West Berlin side of the state border - stood the allied control building. Those from West Germany who wanted to go to West Berlin drove through East Germany, passed Checkpoint Alpha at Helmstedt, 175 kilometers of transit through foreign territory, then Checkpoint Bravo here. The H-shaped control building stood over the roadway. The Dreilinden rest stop nearby: bright red, four stories, a yellow rotating R on the roof. Opened 1989, closed 1990. The buildings still stand, abandoned, south of the exit in the forest.

On the median of Potsdamer Chaussee, 300 meters from this ramp: a memorial grove for the victims of June 17, 1953. No sign on the motorway points to it.
