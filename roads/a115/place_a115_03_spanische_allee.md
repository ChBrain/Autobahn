# Spanische Allee

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn
- Place: [A115](place_the_a115.md) in [Berlin](place_berlin.md)

## Shown
Exit 3. km 18.3. The sign: Spanische Allee. The Grunewald rest stop lies directly at this exit - on the right, gas station, the old AVUS GmbH administration building from 1935, today a rest stop. The Grunewald forest ends here. Behind the ramp: Nikolassee, Zehlendorf, the first residential buildings. Berlin begins again.

- Toward Dreieck Funkturm: [Hüttenweg (2)](place_a115_02_huettenweg.md), 4.5 km.
- Toward Kreuz Zehlendorf: 1.5 km.

## Offers
Exit 3 - the ramp. Spanische Allee, Nikolassee, Zehlendorf. The first city exit after the forest. For drivers from Leipzig or Potsdam: here the through-traffic ends and the city begins.

## Withheld
The south curve. Where the rest stop stands, once stood the banked south turn of the AVUS racetrack - planned 1937, never completed because of the war. The built-up embankment became an American shooting range after 1945. Then a gas station. Then a rest stop. The past is in the ground but not on the sign. The sign shows only: Spanische Allee.

This was also the entrance to West Berlin. Those who came on the transit route from West Germany - through East Germany, Checkpoint Bravo at Dreilinden, then this stretch - drove through West Berlin streets here for the first time again. For some it was daily. For some it was once. The sign never mentioned that.
