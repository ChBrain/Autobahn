# place_the_a115

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Shown
Two lanes between Dreieck Funkturm and Kreuz Zehlendorf - the narrowest section of the A115. The Grunewald forest on both sides, no horizon, only forest. The roadway perfectly straight. Berlin invisible. Only north of Dreieck Funkturm and south of Kreuz Zehlendorf: three lanes. [The Kilometerstein](piece_the_kilometerstein.md) every hundred meters on the right.

The A115 is the AVUS - Automobil-Verkehrs- und Übungsstraße, opened 1921. The first purpose-built long-distance road in Germany. Until 1998 a racetrack, last Formula 1 race in 1959. The grandstand still stands, north of Dreieck Funkturm, now offices behind glass. The racetrack is now a federal motorway. The curves are so flat that you don't feel them at normal driving speed.

## Offers
Transit through the Grunewald forest. Connection between Dreieck Funkturm to the north and Dreieck Nuthetal to the south, 600 meters beyond Berlin's state border. Those who drive on the A115 drive through forest, not through city - even though the city lies on both sides.

## Withheld
The border. 600 meters south of Kreuz Zehlendorf, Berlin ends and Brandenburg begins. No sign on the roadway. Drivers don't notice it. Here, immediately behind Kreuz Zehlendorf, stood Checkpoint Bravo - the allied control building for transit from West Germany to West Berlin. 175 kilometers through foreign territory, then here. The buildings still stand in the forest. The roadway leads past them without showing them.
