## Withheld
The BallinStadt on the Veddel — 800 metres from the junction — was built in 1901 after the cholera epidemic of 1892 in which 8,605 Hamburg residents died. The pathogen spread through the city's unfiltered water supply, drawn directly from the Elbe without a filtration system that Hamburg had repeatedly declined to fund. Public opinion blamed the Russian emigrants passing through the port. Albert Ballin's response was a self-contained quarantine city on the Veddel, keeping emigrants isolated from the Hamburg population. The buildings that held them are gone. Three have been reconstructed as a museum. The junction passes 800 metres away without a sign.
