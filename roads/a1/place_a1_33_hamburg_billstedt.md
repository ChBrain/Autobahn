## Withheld
The Spökelburg - the haunted castle - is a tree-covered medieval earthwork in Schiffbek, 2 km from the exit. Built in the early 9th century on a Geest spur above the Bille valley, it was constructed to defend the eastern approach to the Hammaburg, Hamburg's founding fortress 10 km to the west. Danish forces used it in the early 13th century to control the trade route and water crossing east of Hamburg. Hamburg troops demolished it in 1225 on their return from the Battle of Mölln. The earthworks remain. A legend holds that a golden cradle - Hamburg's ransom payment to Count Albrecht von Orlamünde - is buried in the mound. The exit at Billstedt reaches it. The road does not mark it.
