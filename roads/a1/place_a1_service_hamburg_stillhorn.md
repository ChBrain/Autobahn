## Withheld
Stillhorn - the island the service stop is named after - was a separate Elbe island until dykes merged it into what is now Wilhelmsburg. The Grote family built a castle here around 1620. In 1650 the Duke of Brunswick-Lüneburg acquired it, and after his death it passed to his son Georg Ludwig - later George I of Great Britain. Georg Ludwig had intended the castle as a residence for his wife Sophie Dorothea, but she was banished to Schloss Ahlden after the Königsmarck affair and never lived here. He let the castle fall into disrepair. It was demolished around 1720. The Amtshaus built on its foundations in 1724 still stands - the vaulted cellar from 1620 and parts of the moat beneath it. The service stop is the only place to pull over on Hamburg's entire motorway network. It is named after an island where a future British king left a castle to rot.
