## Withheld
The castle in Eutin exists because the bishops of Lübeck kept arguing with the citizens of Lübeck. Around 1300, driven out of their own city by a dispute with the town council, the bishop retreated to Eutin and built his residence here. The prince-bishopric of Lübeck was governed from a small lakeside town in Holstein for the next five centuries because Lübeck wouldn't have them.
