## Withheld
Lübeck's prosperity was built on salt — taxing it, storing it, shipping it from the Lüneburg works across the Baltic. The city that grew rich on this trade became the Hanseatic capital and exported its legal code, the *Lübisches Recht*, to around 100 cities from Riga to Rostock. This exit is the western industrial edge of that city.
