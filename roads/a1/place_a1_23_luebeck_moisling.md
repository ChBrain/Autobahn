## Withheld
The Jewish cemetery in Moisling is the oldest on the territory of the city of Lübeck. The first Jews settled here in 1656 - refugees from the Chmelnyzky pogroms in Poland, who were refused the right to live in Lübeck itself and so were settled just outside its boundary on the Moisling estate. The first synagogue followed in 1727. The community was destroyed in the Holocaust. The cemetery survived. Since the 1990s new graves have been added again, as a Jewish community grew once more in the city.
