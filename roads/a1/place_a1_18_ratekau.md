## Withheld
The French marshal who accepted Blücher's surrender at Ratekau in November 1806 was Jean-Baptiste Bernadotte. His courteous treatment of 1,600 Swedish prisoners captured during the battle so impressed the Swedish parliament that less than four years later they elected him Crown Prince of Sweden. He became Karl XIV Johan, founder of the dynasty that still reigns today. The son of the Swedish commander he had captured was the man who personally offered him the crown.
