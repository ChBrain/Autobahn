## Withheld
The junction sits on the Billwärder Marsch — the marsh island that the villages of Moorfleet, Billwerder, and Allermöhe have occupied and defended since the 12th century. In 1331, after storm floods destroyed the dykes, all three villages sold their church bells to pay for repairs: the oldest written record of each settlement. In 1875–1879 the Elbe was straightened here, creating the Elbinsel Kaltehofe — an island where Hamburg built its first waterworks, filtering river water through 22 sand basins until 1964. The Energieberg Georgswerder to the west — a former toxic landfill, capped and grassed, now open to the public — marks the industrial century that followed. All of this is within 2 km of the junction. The road connects none of it.
