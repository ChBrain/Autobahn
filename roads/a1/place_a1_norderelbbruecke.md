# Norderelbbrücke

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Shown
The Norderelbbrücke. 411 metres of cable-stayed bridge over the northern arm of the Elbe. The river below. The port infrastructure on both banks. The bridge built in 1963 as the centrepiece of the southern Hamburg bypass - the structure that first connected the Hamburg-Lübeck and Hamburg-Bremen motorways into a continuous route.

## Withheld
On the night of 16-17 February 1962, the Norderelbe rose 3.6 metres above normal flood level and more than 60 dykes broke across the Hamburg Marschlande. The bridge was under construction. Wilhelmsburg, the island directly south of this crossing, lost 222 people - the highest death toll of any Hamburg district. The bridge opened in May 1963. It carries no record of what the river did the year before.
