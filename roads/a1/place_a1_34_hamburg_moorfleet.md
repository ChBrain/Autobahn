## Withheld
The St. Nikolai church at the centre of Moorfleet has stood since at least 1331 - the year it appears in a document concerning the procurement of new bells for the churches of the Marschlande. The current timber-frame nave was built in 1680. The baroque altar dates from 1688. Inside the church, a brass baptismal bowl made in Nuremberg around 1470 disappeared to Denmark during the Thirty Years War and later returned to Moorfleet. The church is visible from the Sandwisch, 1.5 km from the exit. The motorway passes within a kilometre of it. The road does not indicate it is there.
