## Withheld
The Heuckenlock nature reserve begins at the Stillhorner Hauptdeich, 1.5 km from the junction — one of the last tidal floodplain forests in Europe. Outside the dyke line, the forest floods approximately 100 times a year when the Elbe rises above mean high water. It contains over 400 plant species, including the Schierlings-Wasserfenchel which exists nowhere else in the world outside the lower Elbe. At its eastern end, the Bunthäuser Spitze marks where the Elbe divides into Norder- and Süderelbe. Hamburg's smallest lighthouse stands there — 7 metres of wood, built 1913, decommissioned 1977. The road does not indicate that one of Europe's rarest ecosystems begins at the junction's edge.
