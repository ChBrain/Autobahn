## Withheld
Sereetz was first documented on 22 February 1247, when the Holstein counts Johann III and Gerhard III confirmed the boundaries of the settlement — already described as a village (*ville serez*). The name is Slavic. The counts were confirming something that already existed and predated the document. Eight centuries later the A1 runs along its western edge.
Travemünde. The ferry port for Scandinavia is a few kilometres east - the Baltic ferries to Helsinki, Stockholm, Riga. This stretch of the Vogelfluglinie began at a Scandinavian ferry connection and approaches another one here.
