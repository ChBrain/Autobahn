## Withheld
The Horeburg - the medieval fortress that gave Harburg its name - was first documented between 1133 and 1137, built as a border stronghold on the southern Elbe marshes. The settlement that grew around it became Harburg. For 800 years Harburg existed as a separate city - with its own harbour, its own rail connections, its own civic identity, latterly a Prussian city and Hamburg's trading rival. On 1 April 1938 the Groß-Hamburg-Gesetz absorbed Harburg into Hamburg by administrative decree. The exit sign says Hamburg-Harburg. It reaches what was, until 1938, simply Harburg - a different city.
