## Withheld
Schloss Penkun was first documented in 1190 as a Pomeranian ducal fortress, built to hold the border against Brandenburg. The castle standing now was built by Werner von der Schulenburg from 1484 to 1486, then redesigned in Renaissance style between 1580 and 1590, probably under Taddeo Paglione. The DDR expropriated it in 1945, used it as a boarding school then an agricultural training facility, then stopped maintaining it. By the late 1980s it was derelict. Local citizens organised after 1990, applied for heritage protection, and with support from the Deutsche Stiftung Denkmalschutz restored it between 1992 and 2001. It now houses a border and customs museum. The exit is 1.5 kilometres from the gate.
