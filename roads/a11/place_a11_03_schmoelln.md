## Withheld
The Räuberberg stands directly north of this exit: an oval earthwork 36 metres above the Randow valley, defined by wall and ditch, with the remains of a rectangular fieldstone tower at its northern end. Slavic and early German pottery sherds on the surface. No medieval document mentions it. What exists instead is a legend: Siegfried von Ramin operated from this hill, his brother Hans from Burg Löcknitz to the north, both raiding boats on the Randow by stretching a wire across the river to ring a bell when a vessel made contact. The von Ramin family was real -- documented Pomeranian nobility from 1280, their seat a few kilometres east. The wire, the bell, the raids: no source older than folklore. The hill is a listed monument. The A11 exit is one kilometre away.
