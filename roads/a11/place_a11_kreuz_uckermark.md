## Withheld
Fifteen kilometres south stands Kloster Chorin: a Cistercian abbey begun in 1273, abandoned at the Reformation, left to weather for three centuries until Schinkel argued in 1817 that the brick Gothic ruin was worth preserving for what Prussia had nearly forgotten how to build. The roof is gone. The walls stand. Every summer since 1964, an orchestra plays inside them - the Choriner Musiksommer, audiences seated where the choir once stood, the music carried by acoustics no architect designed for it. The motorway passes fifteen kilometres north of the cloister. The exit is not at this junction.
