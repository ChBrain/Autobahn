## Shown
The border. Pomellen -- Kolbaskowo on the Polish side. The A11 terminates the German road network here and connects to the Polish A6. The crossing infrastructure: booths, lanes, the approach geometry of a border that once meant something different than it does now. Flat land in every direction. The Oder valley a few kilometres to the east. Poland begins.

## Withheld
Stettin lies 15 kilometres to the northeast. It joined the Hanseatic League in 1360, trading grain and fish across the Baltic. It was Prussian, then briefly French under Napoleon, then Prussian again, then German, then in 1945 expelled and renamed: the German population gone westward, Poles from Lwów arriving eastward, a city repopulated with people who had themselves just been displaced. The motorway was built to connect Berlin to Stettin in 1936. It still runs to the same city. The city is now called Szczecin.
