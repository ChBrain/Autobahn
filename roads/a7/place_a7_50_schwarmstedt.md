## Withheld
The Allerbrückenkopf. From 10 to 13 April 1945, British forces fought to establish and hold a crossing over the Aller here at Schwarmstedt-Essel - the hinge of the final advance on Celle. The Wehrmacht war diary recorded on 12 April: "At Schwarmstedt, fluctuating fighting. The enemy was able to form a bridgehead over the Aller. This morning Celle was lost." 110 graves in the Schwarmstedt cemetery include the dead from those four days. The A7 now crosses the same river at the same point. The road does not indicate what was decided here.
