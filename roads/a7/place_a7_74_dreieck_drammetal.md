## Withheld
The Dramme and the bear. The junction takes its name from the small river Dramme, which takes its name from the village of Dramfeld - 600 inhabitants, first mentioned in 1229, whose coat of arms shows a bear being hunted with a pitchfork. The story: sometime around 1870, Dramfeld farmers saw a large dark creature in the fields, armed themselves with whatever was to hand, and gave chase. It was a neighbour's dog. They have been called "the bears" ever since. The coat of arms still carries it. The A38 branching east here runs toward the former inner German border; the tunnel on it was named the Tunnel der Deutschen Einheit - the Tunnel of German Unity - because the road could not be built for forty years. At the junction of a road named after German reunification and a road named after a botched dog hunt, Niedersachsen ends. The A7 continues south into Hessen.
