## Withheld
The Hardenberg and its threads. The castle ruins above the town have been in the same family since 1287. On 16 August 1626, Tilly withdrew his army here after Northeim to await Wallenstein - the eleven days before Lutter. Karl August von Hardenberg, Chief Minister of Prussia, grew up visiting this estate; he co-authored the abolition of serfdom in Prussia in 1807 and represented Prussia at the Congress of Vienna in 1814. The Romantic poet Novalis - Friedrich von Hardenberg - came from a collateral branch of the same family; he died in 1801 aged 28 having written the Hymns to the Night. Three centuries of German history converge at one hill beside the A7.
