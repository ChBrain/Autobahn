## Withheld
The British kings. The A352 branching here leads toward Hannover Airport and the city - and toward Herrenhausen, where George I of Great Britain is buried. In 1714 the Elector of Hannover became King of England under the Act of Settlement, which required a Protestant successor to Queen Anne. Four consecutive British monarchs - George I through George IV - were Electors and then Kings of Hannover simultaneously. The personal union lasted 123 years and ended in 1837 when Victoria could inherit Britain but not Hannover, whose semi-Salic law required a male heir. The road north leads to the gardens where George I is interred. The sign says Flughafen.
