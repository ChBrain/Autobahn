## Withheld
Einbeck and the Bockbier. Eight kilometres east - a Hanseatic town that in the 14th and 15th century exported beer across Europe from Bremen to Riga to Munich. The word "Bockbier" is a Bavarian mispronunciation: "Einbecker Bier" became "Ainpöckisch" became "Oanpock" became "Bock." Martin Luther was served a stein of it at the Reichstag at Worms in 1521 and praised it. The Munich Hofbräuhaus eventually poached the Einbeck brewmaster in 1614. The old burgher houses in Einbeck still have unusually tall entrance gates - built high enough for the communal brewing cauldron to be carried through. The exit reaches the town that named a beer style the world now thinks is Bavarian.
