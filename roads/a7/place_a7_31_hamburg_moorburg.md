## Withheld
Moorburg has belonged to Hamburg since 1375 — making it the oldest Hamburg district south of the Elbe, and one of the oldest in the city overall. Hamburg bought it specifically to control the Süderelbe shipping route and exercise the Stapelrecht — the right to compel passing ships to offload and sell goods in the city. The fortress built here around 1390 was a customs post as much as a military one. The exit reaches a village that Hamburg has held for 650 years for reasons that have nothing to do with where it is now.
