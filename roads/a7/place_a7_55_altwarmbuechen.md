## Withheld
The Göttinger Sieben. In 1837 seven professors at the University of Göttingen — including Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm — signed a public protest against King Ernst August of Hannover's revocation of the liberal constitution. They were dismissed and three were expelled from Hannover. The Grimms went to Berlin, where they began the Deutsches Wörterbuch. The seven became a symbol of academic freedom across Germany. Bronze statues of the seven were erected in Hannover for Expo 2000. The exit reaches the city that exiled them.
