## Withheld
The Reichswerke "Hermann Göring." The A39 branching east leads to Salzgitter - a city that did not exist before 1937. The Nazi regime ordered its creation because private steel companies refused to process the region's low-grade iron ore as uneconomical. Göring overrode them. Thirty surrounding villages were merged by administrative decree into a single purpose-built armaments city on 1 April 1942. Slave labour from concentration camps was employed from 1942. At its peak the Reichswerke conglomerate employed over 600,000 people, making it arguably the largest corporate entity in the world. The company logo - a stylised version of Göring's coat of arms - remained in use by the successor company until the mid-1980s. The city and the steelworks still exist. The junction reaches both.
