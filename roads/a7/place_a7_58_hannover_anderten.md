## Withheld
The Maschsee. Four kilometres west - a 78-hectare artificial lake dug entirely by hand from 1934 to 1936. No machines were used except transport wagons: the Nazi regime wanted the image of unemployed men working, not machinery. 1,650 workers excavated 780,000 square metres of earth at wages barely above unemployment support. The opening ceremony on 21 May 1936 was a major propaganda event with hundreds of thousands attending. The sculptures on the north shore - including bronzes by Hitler's favourite sculptor Arno Breker - remain and still generate debate. Hannover's most popular recreation lake was built as a political performance. The exit reaches it.
