## Withheld
Schloss Derneburg. Four kilometres west - a 12th-century monastery converted to a castle, most recently an old people's home, bought in 1975 for 300,000 DM by the painter Georg Baselitz. He lived and worked here for 31 years. In this building he first became a sculptor, carving his earliest sculptures from trees felled in the surrounding forest. In 2006 he sold the collection and then the castle to the American collector Andrew Hall, who restored it as the Hall Art Foundation - now one of the largest private contemporary art museums in Europe. The exit reaches it.
