## Withheld
Kloster Brunshausen. Ten kilometres east near Bad Gandersheim - in 852 the Saxon count Liudolf, progenitor of the Ottonian dynasty, founded the Gandersheim women's foundation on his estate here. The family that grew from this act produced Otto I, the first Holy Roman Emperor. At the same foundation in the 10th century, the canoness Hrotsvit of Gandersheim wrote six plays in the style of Terence - the first known dramatic works composed in the West since antiquity, and the earliest known literary works by a German woman. The exit reaches both the cradle of the Ottonians and the first post-antique playwright in one building.
