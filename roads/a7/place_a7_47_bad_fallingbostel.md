## Withheld
The grave of Hermann Löns. In the Tietlinger Wacholderhain near Walsrode - reachable from this exit. Löns died in 1914 at the Western Front and was buried in France. In 1934 the Nazis exhumed what they believed were his remains and brought them to Germany for a state burial. The original site chosen was the Sieben Steinhäuser - the same Neolithic stone graves where the Dorfmark farmers had met that same year to resist the Wehrmacht training ground. The training ground made both plans impossible. SA men stored the coffin temporarily in a hotel garage. It was eventually buried in the Tietlinger heath in 1935. The grave is a Naturschutzgebiet. The exit reaches it.
