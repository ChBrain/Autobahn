## Withheld
Otto von Northeim. The town takes its name from his family. He was Duke of Bavaria from 1061 - the most powerful Saxon magnate of his generation - until Heinrich IV had him charged in 1070 with plotting a royal assassination, an accusation almost certainly fabricated. When Otto refused to appear for trial without a guarantee of safe conduct, he was stripped of Bavaria, outlawed, and imprisoned. He was eventually released, recovered his personal estates, and spent the rest of his life leading the Saxon aristocratic opposition against Heinrich. He died on 11 January 1083 having never recovered his dukedom. The Investiture Controversy - the defining crisis of 11th-century German politics - ran partly through this town.
