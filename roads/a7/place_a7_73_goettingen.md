## Withheld
The Georgia Augusta. Founded 1737 by George II of Britain as Elector of Hannover - one of the first universities to place all faculties on equal footing, freeing research from theological censorship. Napoleon called it "the university of Europe." The Humboldt brothers, Bismarck, and Heine all studied here. By the early 20th century Göttingen was the world centre of mathematics and physics. In 1933 over fifty professors were dismissed - among them Max Born and Emmy Noether - and that world ended. The university never recovered its former position. In the market square stands the Gänseliesel, a bronze goose-girl traditionally kissed by every new doctoral graduate. The city eventually had to forbid it. The kiss outlasted everything else.
