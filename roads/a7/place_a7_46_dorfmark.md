## Withheld
The displaced villages. To the east, the Truppenübungsplatz Bergen - the largest military training ground in Germany. To establish it in 1935-36, the Wehrmacht cleared 24 villages and resettled approximately 3,650 people against their protests. The farmers met in October 1934 at the Sieben Steinhäuser - Neolithic burial grounds within the training area - to organise their delegation to Berlin. They lost. The road running alongside the A7 here is the 1936 military bypass; the original B3 alignment survives inside the training ground, used for tank exercises. The administrative procedures for dissolving the municipalities were never legally completed. The Heimatverein Dorfmark holds the resettlement files.
