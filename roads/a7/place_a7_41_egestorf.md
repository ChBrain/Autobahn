## Withheld
Pastor Wilhelm Bode. He served as pastor in Egestorf from 1886 for 37 years and spent that time buying the Heide back from the Prussian state. In 1906 he acquired the Totengrund. In 1909 he founded the Verein Naturschutzpark in Munich, modelled on the American national parks. In 1910 the Wilseder Berg was purchased. In 1921 the Prussian government declared the area a Naturschutzgebiet - the first protected landscape of its scale in Germany. A bust of Bode stands a few metres from the Egestorf church. The protected, car-free heath the driver has been passing through for the last ten kilometres exists because of what he did from this village.
