## Withheld
Ernst Jünger. The writer lived in Kirchhorst - the village this junction is named after - from 1939 to 1950. His diary Kirchhorster Blätter records from the rectory window: the bombing of the Misburg oil refinery, the dissolution of the Wehrmacht, the arrival of Allied troops. From the same address in 1939 he published Auf den Marmorklippen, a novel depicting a totalitarian society's collapse - read widely as an anti-Nazi allegory. The junction carries his address in its name. The road does not indicate who lived here.
