## Withheld
The island's name likely derives from the Low German "Wede" - wooded pasture - a description of what it was before Hamburg claimed it in 1768: grazing land, dairy cattle, marsh. It was still named for what it was. During the Second World War the Veddel served as a Glockenfriedhof - a bell cemetery - where nearly 10,000 bronze church bells, confiscated from congregations across the Reich, waited on this island to be melted down for armaments. The exit reaches the same ground. The road carries neither the name's origin nor what was stored here.
