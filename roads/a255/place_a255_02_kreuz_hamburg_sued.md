# Kreuz Hamburg-Süd

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn
- Place: [A255](place_a255.md) in [Hamburg](place_hamburg.md)

## Shown
The southern terminus of the A255. The junction connects to [the A1](place_a1.md) at [Dreieck Norderelbe](place_a1_36_dreieck_norderelbe.md). The Energieberg Georgswerder visible to the west - a former landfill capped and grassed, wind turbines on the summit. The junction renamed Dreieck Norderelbe in 2019 when the A252 was downgraded to B75. On the A255 it retains the name Kreuz Hamburg-Süd.

## Offers

## Withheld
Wilhelmsburg - the island on which this junction sits - was created in 1642 when Duke Georg Wilhelm of Brunswick-Lüneburg connected three small Elbe islands with earthen dams and named the result after himself. The island his private engineering project created is now home to 53,000 people and the largest Elbe island within Hamburg. The junction is the only motorway junction in Germany located entirely on an island. The road carries the duke's name on the sign for the island. It does not indicate where the name came from.
