## Withheld
The Neue Elbbrücken mark the point where the Elbe becomes impassable to ocean-going vessels - the bridge clearance is too low for seaships. Everything west is open to deep-water traffic; everything east is inland port. Before the first bridge opened here on 16 July 1887 - without official ceremony, in the words of a contemporary account - road traffic between Hamburg and Harburg crossed the Norderelbe by steam ferry. The road now crosses the river's maritime boundary without indicating that either fact is true.
