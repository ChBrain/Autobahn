# A255

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Shown
The Abzweig Veddel. 2 kilometres of motorway connecting the Neue Elbbrücken to the A1 at Dreieck Norderelbe. Three lanes. The road runs south through the Veddel island - a narrow strip of land between the Norderelbe and the Spreehafen, the port on both sides. The city centre 2 km to the north across the water. The A1 freight corridor 2 km to the south. The A255 is the connector between them.

## Offers
The connection between the Neue Elbbrücken and the A1. The only motorway access to the Veddel. Three lanes throughout - no speed limit on most sections.

## Withheld

**1. The port**
Present as truck density and freight volume on every approach road. Invisible from the carriageway. The Köhlbrandbrücke - the port's own crossing - visible briefly from the A7 south of the Elbtunnel, 2 km to the east, then gone. The container terminals, the bulk cargo quays, the drydocks: all within 3 km of the A7 at Waltershof. None of it visible from the road. The port is the reason the road exists here. The road does not show it.

**2. The water**
Hamburg is built on rivers. The motorway network crosses the Elbe twice - once underground at the Elbtunnel, once on the Norderelbbrücke at Moorfleet - and the Alster not at all. The A7 passes under the Elbe without a view. The A1 crosses the Norderelbe on a cable-stayed bridge at 10 metres above the water - the river visible for approximately 4 seconds at motorway speed, then gone. The Außenalster, the city's defining inland water: not crossed by any motorway. Not visible from any motorway. Entirely withheld.

**3. The skyline**
Hamburg has a recognisable skyline - the Elbphilharmonie, the Michel, the Speicherstadt roofline, the five main church towers that historically defined the city's profile. None of it visible from the motorway network. The A7 runs in a trench through the western city. The A1 approaches from the east through Billstedt - industrial fringe, no view. The A255 runs through the Veddel island with the city 2 km north, the Elbphilharmonie visible only from the Neue Elbbrücken at the road's terminus, briefly, before the carriageway descends. A city whose shape from the outside is internationally known. A motorway network that never shows it.

**4. The departures**
Over 5 million people left Europe through Hamburg between 1850 and 1939. The BallinStadt emigration halls on the Veddel island - where emigrants were held, processed, and embarked - sit 800 metres from the A255. No sign from the road. The Veddel exit offers access to the island. The halls are there. The road does not indicate them. The traffic on the A255 now moves in the opposite direction to those departures: into the city, toward the port, redistributing through the Dreiecke. The direction of the journey has reversed. The road carries no memory of the earlier one.

**5. The city that isn't there**
Hamburg has no motorway through its centre. The ring roads distribute traffic around it. The Innenstadt - the Rathaus, the Binnenalster, the Mönckebergstrasse, the entire commercial and civic core - is unreachable from the motorway network without leaving it and navigating ordinary roads. A driver can enter Hamburg on the A7, transit the city completely, and exit on the A1 without ever being closer than 4 km to the city centre. The city's own motorway network does not go to the city.
