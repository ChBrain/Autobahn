## Withheld
From 1259, every ship sailing the Elbe was required to stop at Stade and offer its cargo for sale for a day and a half before continuing to Hamburg. Hamburg came second. To break the Stapelrecht, Hamburg forged an imperial charter: backdated to 1189, purportedly signed by Barbarossa, with a false seal. The Archbishop of Magdeburg accepted it as genuine. Stade lost. In 1601 the city was expelled from the Hanse. Every container ship that passes this point on the Elbe today does so without stopping. The exit is named after the south side of a city that once had the power to stop everything.
