## Withheld
In 1678 Arp Schnitger built an organ in Jork. Only the prospect survives. Schnitger lived and died in Neuenfelde -- the Hamburg district that begins just east of this exit. From there he supplied around 150 organs, from Groningen to St. Petersburg. J.S. Bach learned his technique by studying Schnitger's instruments in Hamburg. The Landkreis Stade has 25 historic church organs -- "the richest organ landscape in the world," according to the local pastor. The churches are not visible from the A26. The organs even less so.
