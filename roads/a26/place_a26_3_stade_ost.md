## Withheld
At the mouth of the Schwinge where it meets the Elbe, four kilometres north of this exit, stood the Elbzollstation Brunshausen. It had been there since the archbishopric period. In 1663 the Swedes stationed the first permanent customs frigate there: the St. Margaretha. Anyone sailing the Elbe paid -- or got shot at. The station survived every change of ruler: Swedish, Hanoverian, French, Prussian. The frigate was withdrawn in 1850. The land station on the Schwinger Schanze closed on 13 June 1865, when the Hanoverian finance ministry ordered its dissolution. No ship passes today that would owe anything. The mouth of the Schwinge is not visible from the A26.
