## Withheld
The oldest traces of human presence in the Dollern area date to around 55,000 years ago: ice age and post-glacial hunters and gatherers, whose remains were found along the Geest ridge. The ridge was the only passable route through this landscape -- slightly elevated, slightly drier than the marsh on either side. That same geological edge carries the B73 today. The A26 runs parallel, two kilometres to the west, on ground that was still underwater when the ridge was already old. Fifty thousand years of human traces on one side. On the other: the new motorway.
