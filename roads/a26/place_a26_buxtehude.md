## Shown

## Withheld
In 1285 Archbishop Giselbert von Brunkhorst founded Buxtehude as the first German city planned from the outset around a central harbour basin. Dutch engineers built the Fleth: an inner-city canal that backs up the Este and routes it through the town. Until 1962 barges were still unloaded there. The Fleth still exists. The city was designed as infrastructure. The A26 exit was designed as infrastructure for the city. Both stand. Neither is currently in operation.
