## Withheld
West of the exit, the Jungfrauenweg runs through the forest: an old track, named after the Jungfernborn, a spring that rose at a hollow in the path. The legend explaining the name goes like this: the pirate Godeke Michels -- one of the Victual Brothers, Stortebekers's confederate -- rode through the heath here after a raid on the Weser. At the spring sat a girl in a white dress, drawing water with a golden cup. Godeke wanted the cup. The girl refused him. He ordered her taken -- and she vanished, and with her the cup and the water of the spring. A family still fetched their drinking water from the Jungfernborn until March 1953, when Neu Wulmstorf was connected to the mains supply, and the spring ran dry. The real reason lay several kilometres north: Hamburg's waterworks had been draining the Harburg hills aquifer since the 1920s. The waterworks denied responsibility. The evidence was that they quietly paid farmers compensation for cattle troughs that had run dry. In 2004 the hollow was levelled when the track was widened for refuse collection. The last depression where the spring had been was pushed away. The name of the path remained.
