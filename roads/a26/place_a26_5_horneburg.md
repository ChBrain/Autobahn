## Withheld
In 1311 Heinrich von Borch, known as "de Isern Hinnerk" -- the Iron Heinrich -- fled to the Horneburg, pursued by a coalition of the Archbishop of Bremen, the Duke of Luneburg, and the Bishop of Verden. His first castle at Dannsee was already in ruins. The Horneburg was besieged. He escaped again, was captured on the run through the moor, and imprisoned for five years at Vorde. The burgher knights who had sheltered him were expelled as punishment but allowed to return. In 1316 the cathedral chapter declared the Archbishop insane and removed him from office. Hinnerk was freed. A bronze statue at the Burgmannshof shows the moment the legend describes: lapwings crying out and betraying his hiding place in the peat.
